## 0. Headline items (read first)

### 0.1 The applied change was 1→2 replicas, not the recommended 1→6

fleetlab's recommendation (`evidence/i6/recommendation/`) sizes 6 replicas to the 189.0362 rps
demand. What inferops actually applied and re-measured (IO-T009) was **1→2** replicas — a
disclosed, compose-substrate resource/time-budget scope reduction, compounded by RQ-14 (no
Kubernetes pod scheduling exists in this environment at all). **The 6-replica prediction is
therefore not tested by this evidence — only extrapolated from 1- and 2-replica data**, and
every place that number appears says so explicitly. Full detail: `loop-report.md` §2, §4.3.

### 0.2 The fitted capacity figure is confirmed at its fitted point, and diverges away from it — leaning toward fleetlab's own unpublished alternative estimate

fleetlab's published per-replica capacity fit (33.159 rps, from `ib-t010-e2-baseline-1x-sane`)
is confirmed within **+1.3%** when re-measured at the exact rate it was fitted from. At every
higher offered rate this session's evidence covers (50 rps, 189 rps, and the 2-replica 80 rps
saturation check), the measurement tracks 1–5% from inferbench's own **alternative**,
never-published-as-the-recommendation "overload-empirical" estimate (37.925 rps) and 9–13%
from the published baseline-fit figure. This directly answers — leaning toward 37.925, not
33.159 — the open question fleetlab's own G8 holdout report already flagged as unresolved
(`fleetlab/reports/holdout-validation.md` §2a/§3, a documented MISS at 12.6% error). Full
detail: `loop-report.md` §4.1, §4.2.

### 0.3 A measured autoscaling-signal refinement: `in_flight` beats `queue_depth` for this config

fleetlab's recommendation names `inference_queue_depth` (Contract 2 canonical) as its
autoscaling signal, with its own disclosed caveat that it under-reads true overload once
admission sheds instead of queues. The measured signal-comparison experiment (real Prometheus
+ real `docker stats`, IO-T009) found `inference_requests_in_flight` fires 6.1s after the true
capacity knee with zero false triggers, while `inference_queue_depth` fires **64.4s late** —
sharpening FL-T009's own caveat from a magnitude problem into a stability problem (the shallow
cap-3 queue flickers rather than holding a stable elevated reading right at the knee). A
concrete, measured refinement — not a contradiction of FL-T006's own guidance. Full detail:
`loop-report.md` §4.6, §5.

### 0.4 Comparability-rule audit: this is an independent replication, not a byte-identical re-run

This session directly compared fleetlab's own training-data manifests against IO-T009's
re-measurement manifests. The admission and mock-timing flags match byte-for-byte; the gateway
build (dev commit vs. a later v0.1.0 released digest), host, warm-up policy (50-request
discard vs. none), repetition count (3 vs. 1), and workload seed identity (fleetlab's own
`re_measurement` plan specified seed `10010201`; IO-T009 used freshly authored workloads with
different seeds embedding the same declared rates) all differ. Recorded here so every
percentage in the loop report is read correctly: independent-replication evidence, not
exact-repeat evidence. Full detail: `loop-report.md` §3.1.
