# The I6 loop report — predicted vs measured, published honestly

**Scenario E / IL-T006.** Assembled 2026-07-12. This is the central integration story of the
program: `inferbench` benchmark results → `fleetlab` capacity recommendation → `inferops`
applies a change → `inferbench`-class re-measurement → predicted vs measured, published
**including where the prediction was wrong**. Per this repo's role (loop owner, not
recommender/applier/measurer — those are fleetlab/inferops), every number below is a citation
to evidence already published in a sibling repo or newly generated by this session's own
independent re-validation; nothing is re-derived or narrated from memory.

## 0. Headline verdict

**The loop closes.** A real recommendation was produced before any change, a real change was
applied, a real repeated measurement was taken, and the comparison is published here — nothing
was skipped, nothing was cherry-picked. Three things are surfaced prominently, not buried:

1. **fleetlab's fitted capacity figure is confirmed at the exact point it was fitted from.**
   Re-measuring the identical `admission-sane-v1` config at the identical offered rate
   (37.8072 rps) that trained the fit landed at 33.58 rps achieved — **+1.3%** from fleetlab's
   fitted **33.159 rps/replica**. A strong, independent, cross-environment replication.
2. **Away from that exact point, the prediction and the measurement diverge — and the honest
   answer favors fleetlab's *other*, non-published capacity estimate.** At every offered rate
   measured above the fitted point (50, 189 rps), the measurement tracks 1–5% from
   inferbench's own alternative "overload-empirical" estimate (**37.925 rps/replica**) and
   11–13% from the published baseline-fit figure (33.159 rps). This is exactly the open
   question fleetlab's own G8 holdout report (`reports/holdout-validation.md` §2a, §3) already
   flagged as unresolved (a MISS, documented as a limitation, 12.6% error) — this session adds
   three new, independent measured points, and **all three lean toward 37.925, not 33.159.**
3. **The applied change was not the recommended change.** fleetlab recommended scaling **1→6**
   replicas. What was actually applied and re-measured was **1→2** — a real, disclosed,
   compose-substrate resource/time-budget scope reduction (§2). The 6-replica prediction
   itself was never tested; only extrapolated from 1- and 2-replica data (§4.3). This is
   stated plainly here and in every section that touches it — the 6-replica number is **not**
   a measured result.

A fourth, smaller but real refinement: fleetlab's recommendation names `inference_queue_depth`
as its (Contract-2-canonical, fallback) autoscaling signal; the measured signal-comparison
experiment found `inference_requests_in_flight` **clearly outperforms it** for this exact
shallow-queue (cap 3) admission config — not a contradiction of FL-T006's own guidance (which
already grouped the two as co-equal fallbacks), but a concrete, measured reason to prefer one
over the other for this config class (§4.6).

## 2. The applied change (inferops, IO-T009) — and how it differs from what was recommended

Source: `inferops` (`/home/user/inferops`), commit `f5fdd86ae670b1a1b0d6412f3c530f6e939ebd74`
(IO-T009), HEAD at time of archiving `89871a64d35bab9450481ff1afdd19ad8310a9d9` (a later,
unrelated fault-matrix-count fix); `experiments/autoscaling/results.md`.

**Stated plainly: inferops did not apply the recommended 1→6 scale-out.** It applied and
re-measured **1→2** — `experiments/autoscaling/run-scaling-demo.sh` /
`run-scaling-demo-2replica-capacity.sh` bring up a second `gateway-signals-2` +
`mock-signals-2` container pair behind `haproxy-signals` (wall time: 3s from start to both
targets ready and haproxy healthy). This is a genuine, disclosed scope reduction — recorded by
inferops itself as "6-replica re-measurement not run — resource/time-scoped down to a genuine,
saturating 2-replica check instead of fleetlab's full 1→6 plan" (`results.md` §5) — not a
silent substitution. Two structural reasons compound this reduction:

- **RQ-14 (carried from I5/I7):** this sandbox cannot schedule any Kubernetes pod on any
  CRI-based distribution. The recommended change is enacted as a compose container-count
  change standing in for what a working `ReplicaSet` controller would drive — the same
  substitution already established at I5.
- **HPA authored but not wired to the recommended signal.** `deploy/infergate/base/hpa.yaml`
  (validated against a live k3s API server, `clusters/local/evidence/k3s-validation-20260712-hpa.txt`)
  scales on **CPU `Resource` at 70% target** — a *different* signal than fleetlab's recommended
  `inference_queue_depth`, chosen deliberately as the "naive baseline" per IO-T009's own task
  brief. KEDA (which would let a real controller scale on `inference_queue_depth`) was
  evaluated and **not adopted** (`inferops/docs/adr/0003-keda-not-adopted.md`) because no
  controller of any kind can evaluate any metric in this environment regardless (RQ-14) — so
  standing one up would add operator/CRD surface with zero additional evidence value. **The
  actual 1→2 replica-count change was a scripted/manual trigger, not a live autoscaler
  reacting to the recommended `queue_depth > 1` threshold firing** — the signal-*detection*
  question (§4.6) and the replica-*count* question (§4.1–4.3) were tested separately, not as
  one closed control loop.
- **Contract 7 plumbing gap (loop-mechanics feedback, not a defect):** the recommendation's
  `recommended_topology.replica_groups[0].engine_config.flags` is an **empty object** — the
  admission-sane-v1 flags that actually define "the recommended configuration"
  (`-admission-tenant-queue-cap=3 -admission-global-inflight-budget=6
  -admission-global-queue-cap=3 -admission-queue-deadline=500ms`) live only in the file's prose
  `baseline`/`change_summary` strings, not structurally in `engine_config`. inferops reproduced
  them correctly (byte-identical to `ib-t010-e2-baseline-1x-sane`'s own manifest, confirmed
  §3.1 below) but did so **by reading fleetlab's evidence directly, not by mechanically
  applying `engine_config`** — because there was nothing machine-applicable to apply. This is
  exactly the "loop mechanics broken → fix Contract 7 plumbing" failure-handling clause
  (`scenarios/e/README.md`); recorded here as feedback, not filed as a defect (this repo does
  not patch other repos' contracts).

**What *did* match the recommendation exactly:** the engine/admission configuration
(`gateway-mock-admission-sane-v1`), the model (`mock-8b`), and the hardware class
(CPU-only local-dev-container) — confirmed byte-for-byte in §3.1.

## 3. The repeated benchmark (comparability audit, then results)

### 3.1 Comparability-rule audit — read this before trusting the percentages in §4

The comparability rule (`compatibility/matrix.md`) requires everything held identical except
the single declared variable. This session compared fleetlab's own training-data manifests
(`inferbench/docs/evidence/ib-t010/e2-baseline/rep-1/manifest.json`) against IO-T009's
manifests (`inferops/experiments/autoscaling/evidence/*/inferbench-run/manifest.json`)
directly. **The core engine/admission behavior matches exactly; several other run-condition
dimensions do not** — stated here so the reader can weigh the numbers appropriately, not
discover the mismatch later:

| Dimension | fleetlab's training run (`ib-t010-e2-baseline/-overload`) | IO-T009 re-measurement |
|---|---|---|
| Admission flags | `-admission-tenant-queue-cap 3 -admission-global-inflight-budget 6 -admission-global-queue-cap 3 -admission-queue-deadline 500ms` | **identical**, byte-for-byte (`run-signal-comparison.sh` line 46–47) |
| Mock timing flags | `-ttft 80ms -itl 10ms -error-rate 0` | **identical** (`-ttft=80ms -itl=10ms -error-rate=0`) |
| Gateway build | dev-build `gateway-bin` @ commit `6827d8c3d177464c17fae3b4dc6c2c475323333b` | infergate **v0.1.0 release digest** `sha256:1971426b39…` — a different, later, tagged build |
| Mock engine build | dev, commit `6827d8c` | released digest — same relationship as above |
| Workload identity | named `ib-t010-e2-baseline`/`ib-t010-e2-overload`, seed `10010202` (baseline) | new workloads authored for this task (`autoscaling-signal-comparison-ramp@1.0.0` seed `20260712101`, etc.) — **embeds the same two declared rates as ramp phases, not a byte-identical replay of fleetlab's own seeded schedule** (which specified seed `10010201` in its `re_measurement` block) |
| Repetitions | 3 | 1 |
| Warm-up policy | `discard-requests`, 50 requests | `none` |
| Host | inferbench's own build/run host | inferops's own compose stack — **a different host** (disclosed by IO-T009 itself as deliberate: "independently reproducing the setup rather than reusing inferbench's numbers") |
| Auth mode | not explicitly set in the launch script | explicit `-auth-mode=none` (required so `inferbench` can drive it — the same no-auth-topology constraint already recorded at I7 §0.3) |
| `gateway.config_version` label | `"admission-sane-v1"` | `"flags-v1 (static flag config, -auth-mode=none)"` — a **cosmetic label mismatch only**; the underlying flags are identical (verified above) |

**Conclusion: this is not a strict, single-variable comparability-rule-compliant re-run of
fleetlab's exact recorded training data.** It is best described as an **independent
reproduction** under matched admission/engine/model/hardware configuration and matched
declared offered rates, on a different (later, released) gateway build, a different host, with
no warm-up and a single repetition instead of three. That is a real, positive form of evidence
— an independent environment landing within 1.3% of a fitted figure is, if anything, a
*stronger* signal than a repeat run on the same host would be — but it is not the literal
`re_measurement` plan fleetlab's own recommendation specified (which named seed `10010201`
specifically). The percentages in §4 should be read as "independent replication," not "exact
re-run."

## 4. Predicted vs measured

### 4.1 Single-replica capacity — the direct test of fleetlab's fitted figure

| Measurement | Offered rps | Duration | Measured goodput rps | vs fleetlab's 33.159 (baseline-fit, **published**) | vs inferbench's 37.925 (overload-empirical, **not published as the recommendation**) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Signal-comparison phase 3 (exact IB-T010 E2 **baseline** rate — the fitted point) | 37.8072 | 60s | **33.58** | **+1.3%** | −11.5% |
| Signal-comparison phase 4 (exact IB-T010 E2 **overload** rate) | 189.0362 | 15s | **37.40** | +12.8% | **−1.4%** |
| Scaling-demo BEFORE (1.5× baseline rate, a third independent point) | 50 | 60s | **36.71** | +10.7% | **−3.2%** |

**Verdict: confirmed exactly at the fitted point (+1.3%); the miss grows monotonically with
distance from the fitted point, and consistently in the direction of the unpublished
overload-empirical estimate.** This is not a refutation of the published 33.159 rps figure —
it is the correct number at the load level it was measured at — but it is a concrete,
three-point answer to the open question `holdout-validation.md` §2a/§3 already flagged (which
of fleetlab's two single-point estimates predicts better away from that point), and the answer
leans toward 37.925, not 33.159. Token-throughput cross-checks the same story: phase 3's 259.6
measured tok/s vs. inferbench's own 260.4 tok/s at the identical rate (**−0.3%**); phase 4's
297.0 vs. inferbench's 282.0 (**+5.3%**, same direction as the rps gap).

### 4.2 Two-replica capacity — a genuine, saturating linear-scaling check

| | Value |
|---|---|
| Offered rps (client-observed) | 79.39 |
| **Measured 2-replica goodput** | **72.39 rps** |
| Shed fraction (confirms capacity-capped, not demand-capped) | 8.81% |
| Linear 2× prediction from fleetlab's 33.159 | 66.32 (observed **+9.2%** above) |
| Linear 2× prediction from inferbench's 37.925 | 75.85 (observed **−4.6%** below) |

The observed ceiling lands **between** the two candidate predictions, closer to the
overload-empirical-based one — consistent with §4.1's pattern. Linear replica-scaling — an
assumption the recommendation explicitly discloses as untested — holds reasonably well at this
small scale (within ±9% either direction), a real, positive, if partial, answer to that
disclosed open question.

**A separate, non-saturating 1→2 scaling run** (`results.md` §3, 50 rps sustained) showed
`queue_depth` dropping 91% (1.33→0.11) and shed fraction dropping 26.5%→0% the instant the
second replica joined — a real, directly observed signal-drop + recovery story — but **this
run does not itself test capacity scaling**: 50 rps was below 2-replica capacity both before
and after scaling (demand-capped, not capacity-capped), a distinction inferops's own report
flags explicitly to avoid a false "2×34.71 predicted vs 49.96 observed" misreading. The 80 rps
run above is the one that actually tests the ceiling.

### 4.3 The 6-replica recommendation itself — NOT measured, extrapolation only

**This is the headline honesty item.** fleetlab recommended 6 replicas to serve 189.0362 rps
demand, predicting goodput in **[165.279, 189.036] rps**. **No 6-replica measurement exists
anywhere in this evidence.** The compose substrate was scaled to 1 and 2 replicas only
(resource/time budget, §2); RQ-14 already establishes no real scheduler exists in this
environment to enact 6 replicas as pods regardless.

Two extrapolations (not measurements) from the 1- and 2-replica data, reported exactly as
extrapolations and nothing more:

- 6 × 33.58 (phase-3 single-replica measurement) = **201.5 rps**
- 6 × 36.0 (average of the three single-replica measurements) ≈ **216 rps**

Both land comfortably inside or above fleetlab's stated `[165.279, 189.036]` interval —
consistent with, not contradicting, the recommendation — **but this is an extrapolation from
1–2 real replicas, not a 6-replica result, and every use of it in this document and elsewhere
must say so.** The recommendation's own `re_measurement.success_criteria` (whether the
6-replica figure lands nearer 199.0 rps from the baseline-fit or 227.6 rps from the
overload-empirical prediction) **remains open** — it cannot be answered from this evidence.

### 4.6 Autoscaling signal — a measured refinement, not a contradiction

fleetlab's recommendation names `inference_queue_depth` (Contract 2 canonical) as its signal,
explicitly disclosing that it "under-reads true overload once admission control starts
shedding instead of queuing." IO-T009's signal-comparison experiment (real Prometheus + real
`docker stats`, same admission-sane-v1 config, 210s 5-phase ramp) measured five candidate
signals against the workload's real capacity knee:

| Signal | First fire (elapsed s) | Lag vs. true knee (90s) | Early/false trigger? |
|---|---|---|---|
| `inference_requests_in_flight` | 96.1 | **+6.1s** | No — **winner** |
| `inference_queue_depth` (fleetlab's recommended signal) | 154.4 | **+64.4s** | No, but very late |
| token-arrival rate | 55.9 | −34.1s | **Yes** |
| CPU utilization (gateway) | 51.9 | −38.1s | **Yes** |
| CPU utilization (mock backend) | 51.9 | −38.1s | **Yes** |

`queue_depth` fired 64.4s **late** — confirming FL-T009's own disclosed caveat, and sharpening
it: this config's queue is genuinely shallow (cap 3, 500ms deadline) and sheds rather than
queues, so `queue_depth` **flickers** between 0 and a few at the knee instead of holding a
stable elevated reading; it only becomes a *stable* 5-second-sustained signal once the system
is deep in overload — a **stability** problem, not just a **magnitude** problem.
`in_flight_requests` fired 6.1s after the true knee with zero false triggers. **Refinement fed
back to fleetlab (§5): for admission configs with a shallow queue cap, prefer
`inference_requests_in_flight` over `inference_queue_depth` as the fallback autoscaling
signal** — this does not contradict FL-T006 (which already grouped both as co-equal
fitted-profile-independent fallbacks), it resolves which of the two is better for this
concrete config class, with a measured mechanism.

## 6. Uncertainties / open follow-ups

  important open item. Closing it requires either a real Kubernetes scheduler in this
  environment (RQ-14 resolution) or a larger compose-substrate budget for a 6-way container
  scale-out; neither exists as of this session. `fleetlab/emit/dry_run_validate.py` (the
  fleetlab-side mechanical checker for exactly this comparison) exists and is tested against a
  synthetic fixture but **has not been run against any real post-change data** — recorded
  honestly as still open, not attempted by this session (constructing a real Contract-3
  `benchmark-result` file from IO-T009's raw events was judged out of this task's assembly
  scope; a genuine follow-up, not a silent gap).
- **This evidence is not a strict comparability-rule-compliant re-run** of fleetlab's own
  training data (§3.1) — different gateway build, different host, no warm-up, single
  repetition, freshly authored (not byte-identical-seed) workloads. The percentages in §4 are
  independent-replication evidence, not exact-repeat evidence; this is stated once here and
  should be read into every number above.
- **The replica-count change was scripted/manual, not driven by a live autoscaler evaluating
  the recommended `queue_depth > 1`-for-60s threshold** (§2) — the signal-detection question
